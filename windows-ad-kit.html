4 Flight Plan — 14 Days, 3 Gates

Daily routine: check leads, log them in the CRM, glance at spend. Total time: 10 minutes. Do NOT touch the campaign outside the gates — edits reset learning.

Days 1–3

Hands off. Meta is exploring. CPL will look scary on day 1 — ignore it. Log every lead: name, phone valid? zip in service area? answered when called?

Day 3 Gate

Kill obvious losers only. If an ad has spent $8+ with CTR under 0.8% and zero leads while others have leads — pause that ad. Otherwise leave everything alone.

Day 7 Gate

Midpoint verdict. CPL ≤ $50 → keep running, change nothing. CPL over $50 → pause the weakest 2 ads, and if the landing page has 100+ visits with under 5% conversion, the page is the problem, not the ads.

Day 14

Stop ads. Final scorecard. Pull the numbers: total spend, total leads, valid-phone rate, CPL by ad angle. The verdict below decides the next move.

5 The Verdict — What the Numbers Decide

A "qualified lead" = valid phone number + zip in service area + real project intent (windows count & timeline filled honestly). Junk leads don't count toward CPL math.

GREEN — Scale

CPL ≤ $35

Model validated. Sell the leads at $75–150, sign the first buyer, raise budget to $20–30/day, and update the pitch deck with REAL numbers. Slide 4 becomes fact.

YELLOW — Iterate

CPL $35–50

Close but not there. Take the winning angle, write 3 new variants of it, tighten targeting, run a second $70 test. Margin still works at $50 if leads sell for $100+.

RED — Rework

CPL > $50 or junk

Don't scale a broken funnel. Diagnose: high CTR + no conversions = page problem. Low CTR = creative problem. Good CPL but junk phones = qualification problem. Fix, then re-test.

6 Lead Handling SOP
  • Within 15 minutes: lead hits your email → log it in the CRM (Prospecting or a "Pilot Leads" pipeline stage).
  • Within 1 hour: forward to the buyer via text + email: name, phone, zip, window count, timeline. Speed is the product — a 2-day-old lead is worthless.
  • Track disposition: did the buyer reach them? Set an appointment? Close? This data is your proof for the NEXT buyer pitch.
  • No buyer yet? Call each lead yourself within the hour: "Thanks for requesting an estimate — I'm confirming your info so the right local pro can call you." Keeps the lead warm AND verifies quality.
Never let leads rot. Every lead that goes uncontacted poisons the pilot data and wastes real ad dollars. If you can't respond same-day during the flight window, pause the campaign.
