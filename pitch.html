The Problem

Home service companies are starving for good leads.

These are high-margin businesses — windows, HVAC, roofing, solar — and their growth bottleneck is always the same: a reliable source of real customers.

Shared-Lead Aggregators

Angi and HomeAdvisor sell the same homeowner to four competitors at once. It's a race to the phone, and everyone loses except the aggregator.

Retainer Agencies

Monthly fees, vanity-metric reports, zero accountability. The agency gets paid whether the phone rings or not.

DIY Advertising

Owners boost posts and burn budgets guessing at Meta ads. No targeting, no funnel, no tracking — money in, nothing out.

The Model

Exclusive leads, sold per lead.

We own the entire funnel — ad campaigns, landing pages, qualification, tracking, and delivery. Buyers pay only for the leads we hand them. Nothing else.

Meta Ads → Custom Landing Page → Qualified Lead → One Buyer's Phone
Exclusive — every lead goes to exactly one buyer. No races, no sharing.
Pay-per-lead — no retainers, no monthly minimums, no long-term contracts.
Qualified — name, phone, zip, project size, and timeline captured before it ever reaches the buyer.

The Market

High tickets make leads cheap.

A full-home window replacement runs $10K–$20K+. One closed job pays for months of leads — which is why home service companies happily pay premium prices for exclusive ones.

Beachhead: Windows. Founder-level insider knowledge of how window deals are sold and closed — we know exactly what a good lead looks like.
Expansion verticals: HVAC, roofing, solar, pest control — same funnel system, new audience and creative.
Constant demand. Homeowners never stop needing these services — the supply of good leads is the only thing that's scarce.
Geography-proof. Every city has the same companies with the same problem. The playbook repeats market after market.
